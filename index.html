About Ryan C Schmidt

Ryan C Schmidt began learning and performing guitar at the age of nine. He started playing steel string acoustic learning to properly read music and performing in church services on a regular basis. He later was introduced to classical guitar where he began training with teachers such as Dr. Jimmy Moore, Dr. Erol Ozsever, and Mr. Rodger Hudson throughout his middle and high school experience. In this time he also performed in various high profile guitar performances, such as opening for world renowned classical guitarist Pavel Stiedl.  In addition he performed  in an  Adam Holzman Masterclass amongst many others.

Though focusing on classical guitar, he still plays in church on numerous instruments, with steady interests in rock and country guitar as well. Ryan C Schmidt is currently studying at Middle Tennessee State University Pursuing a Dual Degree in Classical Guitar Performance and Audio Production. He studies with Dr. William Yelverton and often plays in MTSU Guitar Quartets and Ensembles and solo performances.

Why Classical Guitar?

Versatile and Adaptable

Guitar is a unique and versatile instrument. It can be used in a variety of settings and venues such as a large beautiful mansion with many guests or a small secluded open air location with close friends and family. The guitar is a go anywhere and do anything instrument.

Elegant Atmosphere

In times when friends and family are visiting with each other, the guitar can add to a vibrant atmosphere. Unlike a DJ or wedding band, Classical Guitar is not specifically used to draw attention and disrupt from a casual social gathering. Instead, Classical Guitar will add to the elegance of your ceremony or cocktail hour.

Association with Nashville Culture

Aside from Nashville's association with Country Music, the guitar is known to be heavily tied to the identity of the capital of Tennessee. From Gibson's Headquarters and the Famous Gruhn's Guitars, adding the instrument to your special event is just another way to bring some local flavor that guests can hear and see!

FAQ

What is required to book a musician?

A signed contract and 50% non-refundable deposit is required to book a musician.

How can we pay for your services?

Preferred payment method is by check, PayPal or Venmo (as long as the client covers the Pay Pal fee).

Do musicians take breaks during long engagements?

For engagements longer than one hour, musicians will be allowed a 10 minute break each hour.

How is overtime handled if my event runs longer than expected?

If you need the musician to stay past the scheduled end time there will be an extra charge for overtime. The rate will be given in the contract. The musician may not be able to stay longer due to subsequent engagements so please book extra time in advance if there's a possibility your event will be delayed.

Do I need to provide audio equipment?

I will provide one amplifier for the guitar.
